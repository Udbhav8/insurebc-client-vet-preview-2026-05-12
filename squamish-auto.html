ICBC Basic Autoplan vs Optional coverage

Basic Autoplan is the mandatory coverage every BC driver must carry. Optional is everything else, and you can buy it from any licensed broker, not just ICBC.

ICBC Basic (mandatory)

  • Third-party liability up to $200,000
  • Accident benefits (medical, rehab, wage loss)
  • Underinsured motorist protection
  • Hit-and-run protection

Optional (your choice of carrier)

  • Collision (damage to your own vehicle)
  • Comprehensive (rockfall, wildlife, theft, weather)
  • Excess Underinsured Motorist up to $1M
  • Loss of Use, Replacement Cost, extended liability

Optional coverage that matters on Highway 99

The Sea-to-Sky Highway has a higher rate of serious crashes, rockfall, and wildlife strikes than most urban routes. These add-ons are worth a look for any corridor driver.

Excess Underinsured Motorist ($1M) - Recommended for daily Hwy 99 drivers. If an at-fault driver carries only Basic, this is the difference between full recovery and a partial settlement.
Replacement Cost - On vehicles less than seven model years old, this covers a new equivalent vehicle in a total-loss claim instead of depreciated value.
Loss of Use - Rental car or transit costs while your vehicle is in the shop. Critical for Whistler and Pemberton residents whose repair shops are in Squamish or Vancouver.
Collision - Damage to your own vehicle regardless of fault. Higher Sea-to-Sky claims frequency makes this worth carrying.
Comprehensive - Rockfall, wildlife strikes (deer, bear on Hwy 99 north of Squamish), tree fall, hail, fire, theft. Common Sea-to-Sky perils.
Trailer & rec vehicle endorsements - Mountain bike racks, ski/kayak racks, utility trailers, motorhomes, and snowmobile/ATV trailers. Many corridor households need at least one.

Is ICBC the only car insurance in BC?
ICBC has a monopoly on Basic mandatory coverage (third-party liability up to $200,000, accident benefits, underinsured motorist). For Optional coverage (collision, comprehensive, extended third-party liability, replacement cost), private carriers like BCAA, Intact (Acerta), Aviva, and others have been competing since 2007. Most Sea-to-Sky drivers do not realize they can buy Optional from someone other than ICBC.
Do I need extra coverage for the Sea-to-Sky Highway?
We strongly recommend Excess Underinsured Motorist coverage up to $1M for anyone driving Hwy 99 regularly. The highway has a higher rate of serious-injury crashes than urban arterials, and the at-fault driver's liability limit caps your recovery if they are underinsured. Comprehensive coverage is also worth carrying for rockfall, wildlife strikes (deer, bear on Hwy 99 north of Squamish), and weather damage.
