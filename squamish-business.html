Whistler tourism operator coverage

Tourism is the corridor's biggest industry and the most under-served by commercial insurance. Heli-ski, snowmobile, cat-ski, mountain bike, kayak, climb, and SUP operators all share the same coverage skeleton: a license to operate, a passenger or participant liability layer, a commercial vehicle fleet, equipment coverage, and weather-driven business interruption.

  • Passenger liability for guests in operator vehicles and shuttles (often $5M-$10M).
  • Equipment rental bailee for customer gear in your care.
  • Weather and avalanche-driven cancellation, plus seasonal staffing EPL exposure.

Squamish & Whistler short-term rental operator package

Squamish and Whistler are among Canada's hottest short-term rental markets. Standard home policies exclude commercial use, and most STR claims that get denied get denied because the host never disclosed the rental. The right package is a landlord property policy plus commercial general liability plus business interruption for lost rental income.

Where BC Short-Term Rental Accommodations Act principal-residence rules apply, we help confirm your operation meets the requirements before the next booking. If you run multiple units across Squamish, Brackendale, or Whistler, we structure the portfolio on a commercial schedule instead of one-off home endorsements.

FAQ

How much does business insurance cost in Squamish?
It depends on the operation. A short-term rental host typically pays $300-$1,500/year for commercial liability and landlord property. A Whistler Village restaurant runs $2,000-$6,000/year including liquor liability. A Sea-to-Sky contractor sits at $1,200-$3,500/year for CGL, with commercial auto and tool coverage on top. A licensed tour operator with passenger liability lands between $1,500 and $8,000+ depending on activity, vehicles, and guide certifications.
Do I need commercial insurance for my Airbnb in Whistler?
Yes. Standard home and condo policies exclude commercial use, so an Airbnb or VRBO booking can void coverage. Most Whistler and Squamish hosts need landlord property coverage plus a commercial general liability policy, and many also add business interruption to protect rental income. We also help confirm BC Short-Term Rental Accommodations Act compliance where principal-residence rules apply.
What insurance does a tour operator need in BC?
Tour operators in BC typically carry commercial general liability ($2M-$5M is common), passenger liability for any vehicles carrying guests, operations liability tailored to the activity (heli-ski, snowmobile, mountain bike, kayak, climb guiding), and equipment coverage for owned gear. Operators usually need a Transport Canada or provincial guide license, and WorkSafeBC for any employees or contracted guides.
Does my home insurance cover my Whistler Airbnb?
No, not by default. Home policies are written for private residential use, and any time you accept paying guests on a short-term basis you have moved into commercial territory. Some carriers offer a specific short-term rental endorsement on a home policy, but most Whistler hosts end up on a hybrid landlord plus commercial general liability arrangement. Disclose the rental use to your broker before the first booking.
How much does liquor liability cost in BC?
For a Whistler Village restaurant or bar, liquor liability typically runs $1,000-$3,500/year on top of the base commercial package, depending on annual liquor sales, hours, late-night programming, and claims history. Single-event liquor liability for a wedding or private function is a separate product and usually runs $150-$400 per event.
What is bailee coverage and do I need it for my rental shop?
Bailee coverage protects you for damage to your customers' property while it is in your care. Whistler and Squamish ski, snowboard, mountain bike, and kayak rental shops need this because the gear you damage during a tune, fitting, or storage is not yours and your standard commercial property policy will not pay for it. Limits typically run $25,000-$250,000 depending on inventory volume.
