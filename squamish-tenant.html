What Sea-to-Sky tenant insurance covers

Tenant insurance covers your belongings and your personal liability. Your landlord's policy only covers the building.

Contents - Furniture, electronics, ski and bike gear, clothes, kitchen, anything you own. Replacement cost is standard.
Personal liability - If you accidentally damage your unit, a neighbour's unit, or injure someone. $2M default in Whistler, $1M in Squamish.
Additional living expenses - Hotel, food, and storage if your unit becomes uninhabitable after a covered loss. Often 20-30% of contents limit.
Identity theft - Most modern tenant policies include identity-theft expense coverage at no extra cost (typically $25,000).

Why your landlord's policy does not cover you

Your landlord's policy covers the building and their personal liability, not yours or your stuff. If a kitchen fire damages your unit and the suite next door, the landlord's insurer will pay for the structure and then come after you (subrogation) for everything they paid out. Tenant insurance is what stops that claim at the liability limit instead of your savings account.

In Whistler the math is unforgiving: a water escape into the strata lot below can run $50,000-$200,000 in damages before you count loss of rental income. A $20/month tenant policy with $2M liability handles that scenario.

Sea-to-Sky tenant scenarios we cover

The corridor rental market is unique: resort staff housing, shared rentals, and seasonal workers all need different policy forms.

Whistler resort staff housing - Whistler Housing Authority (WHA), Whistler Blackcomb and Vail Resorts staff units. Most now require proof of tenant insurance as a tenancy condition. We process the paperwork and name the landlord as additional insured.
Seasonal tenant (Nov-Apr or May-Sep) - Short-term policies for the ski season or summer season. Most carriers will write four to six months. Buy before move-in so the first night is covered.
Shared rental / roommates - Unrelated roommates each need their own policy. Couples or family in the same unit are usually fine on one policy. We help structure the right combination.
Squamish suite or laneway home - Basement suites in Garibaldi Highlands, Brackendale, and Valleycliffe are the corridor's most common rental form. Tenant insurance and the landlord's primary policy work together.

FAQ

How much is tenant insurance in Squamish or Whistler?
Whistler studio or dorm-style staff housing typically runs $15-$18/month. A Whistler 2-bedroom shared rental costs $18-$25/month per tenant. A Squamish 2-bedroom suite or detached unit lands at $20-$28/month. Premium depends on contents value, liability limit, and deductible. $2M liability is the corridor default we recommend in Whistler.
Does Whistler staff housing require tenant insurance?
Most Whistler Housing Authority (WHA) and Whistler Blackcomb/Vail Resorts staff units now require proof of tenant insurance as a condition of tenancy. The standard requirement is contents coverage plus $1M-$2M personal liability with the landlord named as additional insured for property damage caused by the tenant. We process WHA and Vail Resorts paperwork routinely.
Do roommates need separate tenant insurance policies?
Generally yes. Tenant insurance is written per tenant and unrelated roommates need their own policies. One combined policy almost never works because each tenant's contents, liability exposure, and claims history are different. Couples and immediate family in the same unit are usually fine on a single policy.
Is there short-term tenant insurance for seasonal workers?
Yes. We offer short-term tenant policies for ski-season workers (typically November to April) and summer staff (May to September). Most carriers will write a four- to six-month term, sometimes with a small minimum premium. Seasonal workers should buy the policy before move-in so the first night is covered.
How much liability should I carry as a Whistler renter?
We recommend $2M minimum personal liability in Whistler and $1M in Squamish. Whistler liability claims tend to be larger because of high-value adjacent units and proximity to luxury short-term rentals: a tenant-caused fire or water escape can damage neighbouring strata lots and assessments can run six figures. The premium delta between $1M and $2M is usually $20-$40/year.
Does tenant insurance cover my ski or bike gear?
Yes, your gear is part of your contents and is covered against fire, theft, and most insured perils whether it is at home, in the lockup, on the chairlift, or in your car. Some carriers have a per-item limit for sports equipment (typically $1,500-$2,500). High-value items like a $5,000 e-bike or a custom race ski setup should be scheduled as a separate rider with proof of purchase.
