ICBC Basic vs Optional, explained

Every Autoplan policy in BC is two products stacked together. Basic is mandatory and ICBC-only. Optional is where private carriers compete, and where most Victoria drivers can save.

Basic Autoplan (ICBC only)

  • Third-party liability up to $200,000
  • Accident benefits (medical and rehab)
  • Underinsured motorist protection
  • Inverse liability
  • Hit-and-run coverage

Typical Basic premium: roughly $300-$400 per year. Same from every BC broker because ICBC sets the rate.

Optional (ICBC or private)

  • Collision: typical $300-$700/yr
  • Comprehensive: $150-$400/yr
  • Excess Underinsured Motorist ($1M): ~$20/yr
  • Loss of Use, Replacement Cost, New Vehicle Protection
  • Extended third-party liability ($2M-$5M)

Quoted side by side: ICBC vs BCAA Saver vs Intact Acerta vs Aviva. We pick whichever carrier fits the vehicle, driver profile and coverage needs.

Premium factors we check on every Victoria quote

Claim-Rated Scale

Each claim-free year moves you down ICBC's CRS. Drivers with 20+ claims-free years sit at the lowest position on the scale.

Multi-vehicle

Second and third vehicles on the same policy are rated together on Optional coverage, which can change the per-vehicle cost.

Home + auto together

We quote your Victoria home insurance alongside auto so both policies can sit with the carrier that fits best.

Employer & association

UVic, Camosun, BC government, RCMP, BC Nurses and other groups have affinity programs we check on the Optional side.

Pleasure-use class 002

Most retirees and work-from-home Victorians should be class 002 (pleasure), not 007 (to-and-from work). We check this on every renewal.

Higher deductible

Moving collision/comprehensive deductibles from $300 to $1,000 typically lowers the premium by $80-$200 per year per vehicle.

FAQ

How much is car insurance in Victoria, BC?
Most Victoria drivers pay $1,500-$2,400 per year for Basic Autoplan plus Optional coverage. Brand-new commuters in Saanich and the Westshore often see $2,800+ in the first year. Mature drivers with 20+ claims-free years can drop to about $1,100. The Basic portion is the same from any broker because ICBC sets it; Optional is where we shop BCAA Saver, Intact Acerta and Aviva to see if private-market pricing fits the vehicle and driver profile.
How much is car insurance in Victoria monthly?
Most Victoria drivers pay $125-$200 per month on ICBC monthly billing for Basic + Optional combined. New drivers and high-value vehicles can be $235+ per month, mature claims-free drivers can be under $95 per month. ICBC offers monthly payment plans on any policy over $300 in annual premium.
How can I lower my car insurance premium in BC?
Five things actually move the premium needle in BC: (1) compare Optional coverage between ICBC and private-market carriers, (2) raise your deductible from $300 to $1,000, (3) quote home and auto together so we can place both with the carrier that fits best, (4) drop collision on older vehicles where the rebuild cost is below the premium, and (5) confirm your rate class is correct (most pleasure-use drivers should be class 002 not 007). We do all five reviews on every Victoria quote.
Is ICBC the only car insurance in BC?
ICBC has a monopoly on Basic mandatory coverage (third-party liability up to $200K, accident benefits, underinsured motorist). For Optional coverage (collision, comprehensive, extended third-party above $200K, vehicle damage), private carriers like BCAA, Intact (Acerta), Aviva and others have been competing since 2007. Most Victoria drivers do not realize they can buy Optional from someone other than ICBC.
Can I renew my ICBC insurance over the phone?
Yes. If your circumstances have not changed (no new drivers, address or vehicle), we can email you the renewal documents, you e-sign electronically, and we mail your decals to your Victoria address. We process online renewals for hundreds of Greater Victoria residents every month, no office visit required.
What is an ICBC Autoplan?
ICBC Autoplan is BC's combined auto insurance and vehicle registration product. It includes Basic mandatory coverage (third-party liability, accident benefits, underinsured motorist) plus your vehicle registration and licence plates. You can add Optional coverages (collision, comprehensive, excess underinsured motorist, replacement cost, loss of use) either through ICBC or through a private-market carrier via an independent broker.
