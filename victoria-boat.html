Boat & Marine Insurance in Victoria, BC

Boat insurance in Victoria runs $150-$400/year for a dinghy, $400-$1,200 for a runabout, $800-$2,500 for a 30-40' sailboat, and $1,500-$5,000+ for cabin cruisers and yachts. Hull, marine liability, towing and fuel-spill cleanup for Inner Harbour, Oak Bay, Sidney and Salt Spring boaters.

What a Vancouver Island marine policy covers

  • Hull (agreed value preferred over ACV)
  • Machinery (engine, drive, electronics)
  • Marine liability ($1M / $2M / $5M tiers)
  • Uninsured boater protection
  • Wreck removal and emergency towing
  • Personal effects on board
  • Fuel-spill cleanup (mandatory in BC waters)

Liveaboard insurance

Victoria has the largest liveaboard community on Vancouver Island. Standard recreational policies typically exclude liveaboard use; a specific endorsement is required. We arrange liveaboard coverage at the Inner Harbour (Causeway, Empress, Ship Point, Wharf Street), Oak Bay Marina, Sidney North Saanich, and Brentwood Bay.

Liveaboard endorsement

Extends coverage to dwelling-style perils and on-board contents while the vessel is occupied as a primary residence.

Personal contents

Higher contents limits than a recreational policy. Electronics, tools, instruments and gear stored on board.

Dockside risks

Shore-power surges, freeze damage, and dock-line failure are common liveaboard claim types in Victoria's wet winter weather.

Charter & commercial operators

Fishing charters, dive charters, sightseeing operators and day-charter rentals need a commercial marine policy with passenger liability and Transport Canada compliance. Cross-link to business insurance for the broader commercial stack (general liability, business interruption, equipment).

Seasonal lay-up vs in-commission

Most BC boats see a materially lower in-water premium with a seasonal lay-up endorsement (typically October through March), where coverage is reduced to fire, theft and named perils while the vessel is hauled or wintered in place. The trade-off is no navigation coverage during lay-up. We model both options on every quote.

FAQ

How much does boat insurance cost in BC?
Boat insurance in Victoria runs $150-$400 per year for a dinghy or tender, $400-$1,200 for a runabout or bowrider under 25', $800-$2,500 for a 30-40' sailboat, and $1,500-$5,000+ for cabin cruisers and yachts. Personal watercraft (Sea-Doo, Jet Ski) run $200-$500 per year. Liveaboard endorsements, commercial charter operations and high-value yachts get custom underwriting, often through Lloyd's syndicates.
Is boat insurance mandatory in BC?
Boat insurance is not provincially mandated for recreational boats in BC, but it is functionally required: most marinas (Oak Bay Marina, Inner Harbour, Sidney North Saanich, Brentwood Bay) require proof of marine liability before they will rent moorage. Commercial vessels and charter operations are subject to Transport Canada requirements. Most BC boaters carry at least $1M-$2M marine liability plus hull coverage.
Does my boat insurance cover liveaboard use?
Standard recreational boat policies typically exclude liveaboard use (using the vessel as a primary or secondary residence for more than 30-60 nights per year). Victoria has a large liveaboard community in the Inner Harbour, Causeway, Wharf Street and Oak Bay Marina, and a liveaboard endorsement is required. The endorsement covers personal contents on board, dock-side amenities and dwelling-style perils. Carrier appetite varies.
What is agreed value vs actual cash value on a boat policy?
Agreed value pays the full insured amount in a total loss, regardless of depreciation. Actual cash value (ACV) pays replacement cost minus depreciation. Agreed value is the better default on any boat you have invested in, especially sailboats with rigging upgrades, custom interior or restored classics. ACV is sometimes used for older, low-value runabouts. We always offer both and price them side by side.
Does boat insurance cover charter/passenger use?
No, standard recreational boat policies exclude any operation for hire (fishing charters, dive charters, sightseeing, day-rentals). A commercial vessel policy with passenger liability is required, plus Transport Canada commercial vessel compliance and crew documentation. We arrange commercial marine policies for Victoria charter and dive operators.
What marine liability limit should I carry in BC?
$1M marine liability is the absolute minimum for a recreational boat in BC, and most marinas require at least this. $2M is the more typical default. For yachts, multi-passenger cabin cruisers, sailing schools and any commercial operation, $5M or higher is the right answer. Fuel-spill cleanup costs alone in BC waters can exceed $1M, so generous limits cost very little for the protection they buy.
