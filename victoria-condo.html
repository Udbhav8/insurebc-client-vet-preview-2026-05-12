Condo Insurance in Victoria, BC

Strata unit owner coverage from $35-$80 per month in Victoria. Contents, betterments, personal liability, loss assessment and strata-deductible coverage, the four things your strata's master policy does not cover.

Five things your unit-owner policy must cover

  • Contents (furniture, electronics, clothing, bikes)
  • Betterments & improvements (quartz, hardwood, custom millwork)
  • Personal liability ($1M / $2M / $5M tiers)
  • Loss assessment ($50K minimum, $100K+ for downtown high-rises)
  • Strata deductible coverage (match the strata's current deductible)
  • Additional living expenses if your unit is uninhabitable

Strata vs unit owner: who covers what

The biggest misconception in BC condo ownership is that the strata's master policy covers everything inside your walls. It does not. Here is the split.

Strata corporation covers

  • Building structure (walls, roof, foundation)
  • Common property (lobby, hallways, elevators, parking)
  • Fixtures installed at original construction
  • Limited common property assigned to your unit

Unit owner covers

  • Contents (your stuff)
  • Betterments & improvements you or a previous owner installed
  • Personal liability worldwide
  • Loss assessment (your share of large strata losses)
  • Strata deductible coverage (if a loss is traced to your unit)
  • Additional living expenses if displaced

The BC strata deductible crisis

Between 2020 and 2022, BC strata insurance underwent dramatic reform. Carrier capacity tightened, claims rose, and strata deductibles ballooned from a typical $5,000 in 2019 to $25,000, $50,000, $100,000 or even $250,000+ by 2022. This is the single piece of context most generic condo-insurance pages skip.

Higher strata deductibles

Confirm your strata's current deductible from the Form B. Most Victoria buildings sit between $25K and $100K today. Your unit-owner policy needs strata-deductible coverage that matches.

Loss assessment matters now

Large building losses can exceed the strata's master policy limit. The shortfall is assessed against unit owners. We recommend $50K minimum, $100K+ for downtown high-rises and older buildings.

Water-damage prevention

Hoses, water heaters, washing machines and dishwashers cause most strata claims. Many BC carriers now require leak detection or shut-off devices in older buildings.

By building type

Concrete high-rise (downtown) - Lower contents-theft risk, higher water-damage exposure from upstairs units. Loss assessment is critical.
Wood-frame low-rise (Saanich, Esquimalt) - Older stock often means higher strata deductibles and more frequent water claims. Verify the strata deductible amount.
Townhouse complex - Often higher per-unit deductible and more individual responsibility. Some strata corporations require unit owners to insure the building envelope.

FAQ

How much is condo insurance in Victoria, BC?
Condo insurance in Victoria typically runs $35-$80 per month ($420-$960 per year) for strata unit owners, depending on building age, deductible, contents value, and how much loss assessment and strata-deductible coverage you carry. Older buildings with higher strata deductibles, downtown high-rises and units with significant betterments (quartz, hardwood, custom millwork) push toward the upper end.
What does strata insurance cover in BC?
The strata corporation's master policy covers the building structure, common property (lobby, hallways, elevators, parking), and fixtures originally installed at construction. It does NOT cover your contents, your betterments and improvements (anything an owner has added or upgraded), your personal liability, or the strata's deductible if a loss is traced to your unit. Those four are why every Victoria strata unit owner needs their own condo policy.
What is loss assessment coverage and do I need it?
Loss assessment covers your share of an assessment the strata corporation levies against unit owners after a covered loss exceeds the strata's master policy limit. After the 2020-2022 BC strata insurance reforms, large fire and water losses can produce six- and seven-figure assessments split among unit owners. We recommend a minimum of $50,000 loss assessment coverage for Victoria condo owners, $100,000 for downtown high-rises and older buildings.
What is strata deductible insurance?
If a covered loss is traced back to your unit (a burst dishwasher hose, an overflowed tub, a faulty water heater), the strata corporation can charge you the entire strata master policy deductible, even if the damage is mostly to other units and common property. BC strata deductibles ballooned from $5,000 in 2019 to $25,000-$100,000+ by 2022. Strata-deductible coverage on your unit-owner policy pays this if you are found responsible. Carry at least the strata's current deductible amount, ideally with a buffer.
Do I need condo insurance if my strata has insurance?
Yes. The strata's policy covers the building and common property; it does not cover your stuff, your liability, your betterments, your additional living expenses if you are displaced, or the strata deductible you would owe after a loss originating in your unit. A standalone condo policy is the only way to cover these. Most mortgage lenders also require proof of unit-owner insurance.
How much loss assessment coverage should I carry in BC?
A starting point is $50,000 for a unit in a newer, low-rise building with a small strata deductible. For older Victoria buildings, downtown high-rises, or strata corporations with $50,000+ deductibles, carry $100,000+. Loss assessment coverage is inexpensive (often $20-$60 per year for $50,000 of coverage), and after the BC reforms it is the highest-leverage line on a unit-owner policy.
