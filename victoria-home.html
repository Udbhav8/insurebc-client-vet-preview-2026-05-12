What a Victoria home policy covers

  • Dwelling (the rebuild cost of your home)
  • Contents (your stuff, replacement cost preferred over ACV)
  • Detached structures (garage, shed, fence, dock)
  • Personal liability ($1M/$2M/$5M tiers)
  • Additional living expenses if displaced

Coverage breakdown

Building

Rebuild cost of the dwelling and attached structures. Replacement cost is the better default in BC versus actual cash value.

Contents

Your personal property at home and worldwide. High-value items (jewellery, bikes, art) need scheduling.

Detached structures

Garage, shed, fence, gazebo, dock. Typically 10 percent of the dwelling limit; can be increased.

Personal liability

$1M, $2M or $5M tiers. Covers injury or property damage you cause anywhere in the world, plus legal defence.

Additional living expenses

If your home is uninhabitable after a covered loss, ALE pays for temporary housing, meals and storage.

Water damage

Burst pipes are standard. Sewer backup and overland water (atmospheric river, flooding) are separate endorsements every Victoria homeowner should consider.

Common Victoria add-ons

Three endorsements matter on almost every Vancouver Island home policy: earthquake, sewer backup and overland water. A fourth, the wildfire endorsement, matters most on the Westshore and in rural Greater Victoria.

Earthquake

Vancouver Island sits on the Cascadia Subduction Zone with a 30 percent probability of a major event in the next 50 years. Earthquake is excluded from base policies. The endorsement typically adds $300-$1,200 per year and is the single most important add-on for a Victoria homeowner.

Sewer backup

Water damage is the #1 home insurance claim type in BC. Sewer backup covers backflow through floor drains, toilets and basement plumbing, and is excluded by default on most BC policies.

Overland water

Atmospheric river events have made overland water (surface water entering through doors and windows from rising creeks, ditches and storm runoff) a real Vancouver Island risk. Carrier appetite varies by postal code.

Wildfire endorsement

Highlands, Sooke, Metchosin, and parts of Langford and the Westshore are wildfire interface. Standard fire is covered, but the wildfire endorsement adds smoke-only damage, evacuation expense and debris removal beyond standard limits.

FAQ

How much is home insurance in Victoria, BC?
Most Victoria homeowners pay $1,100-$2,400 per year for a detached single-family home and $480-$960 per year for a strata condo unit. The number moves with rebuild value, year built, distance to a fire hydrant, claims history, deductible, and add-ons like earthquake or overland water. Heritage homes in Oak Bay and waterfront properties in James Bay or Cordova Bay sit at the upper end. We quote BCAA, Intact, Aviva, Wawanesa and Square One side by side to find the best fit.
How much is the average home insurance in Victoria?
The Greater Victoria average for a typical detached home with a $1,000 deductible and standard coverage runs about $1,500-$1,800 per year. Strata condo unit owners typically pay $400-$900 per year for contents, betterments, liability and loss assessment coverage on top of strata insurance. Tenants in Victoria pay $180-$340 per year for renters' coverage.

For a Greater Victoria homeowner with no claims, a standard detached home, and a $1,000 deductible, BCAA Saver and Intact tend to be the most competitive on price. For higher-value homes ($1.5M+ rebuild), heritage character homes, or homes in wildfire interface zones (Highlands, Sooke, Metchosin), Aviva, Wawanesa and specialty markets often offer better terms. We run all of them.
Do I need earthquake insurance in Victoria?
Strongly recommended. Vancouver Island sits on the Cascadia Subduction Zone and the Geological Survey of Canada estimates a 30 percent chance of a major rupture in the next 50 years. Standard home insurance does NOT include earthquake coverage. The endorsement typically adds $300-$1,200 per year and covers shake damage, fire following earthquake (only if you also carry the fire-following endorsement on some carriers), and additional living expenses. For most Victoria homeowners the math works out.
Does home insurance in Victoria cover wildfire?
Yes, fire including wildfire is a standard covered peril on a BC homeowner policy. What is often missed is the wildfire-specific endorsement available from some carriers that adds coverage for evacuation expenses, smoke-only damage, and debris removal beyond the standard limits. Homes in interface zones (Highlands, Metchosin, Sooke, parts of Langford and the Westshore) should ask us about the wildfire endorsement and a higher additional living expense limit before fire season.
