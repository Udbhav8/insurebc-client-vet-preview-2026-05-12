What life insurance protects

  • Income replacement for surviving family
  • Mortgage payoff (lender's life or term)
  • Children's future education
  • Final expenses and estate tax
  • Business succession and key-person
  • Critical illness lump sum (separate product)

Four product types

Term Life (10/20/30-year) - Lowest-cost option. No cash value. Best for income replacement, mortgage protection and dependent care during peak earning years.
Whole Life (permanent) - Permanent coverage with a fixed premium and guaranteed cash value. Used for estate planning, business succession and guaranteed permanent coverage.
Universal Life - Permanent coverage with flexible premium and investment component. More complex; best for high-income households or estate-planning use cases.
Critical Illness - Lump-sum payout on diagnosis of covered conditions (cancer, heart attack, stroke). Pairs well with disability income coverage.

FAQ

How much life insurance do I need in BC?
A common starting framework is 10x annual income, plus the mortgage balance, plus any debt, plus future education costs for dependents, minus liquid savings. For most Victoria families with young children that lands between $500,000 and $2,000,000 of term coverage. Single individuals without dependents typically need much less, often just enough to cover debts and final expenses.
What is the difference between term and whole life insurance?
Term life provides a death benefit for a defined period (typically 10, 20 or 30 years) and is the lowest-cost option, with no cash value. Whole life is permanent insurance with a fixed premium and a guaranteed cash value that grows tax-deferred. Term is right for most Victoria families covering income replacement and mortgage. Whole life is more often used for estate planning, business succession or guaranteed permanent coverage.
How much does life insurance cost in Victoria, BC?
Term life for a healthy 35-year-old in Victoria runs about $22 per month for $500,000 of 20-year term. At 40 the same policy is approximately $40 per month, and at 50 about $95 per month. Whole life for a 35-year-old at $250,000 is roughly $220 per month. Smokers pay 2-3x non-smoker rates. Pre-existing conditions, family history and lifestyle (recreational pilot, motorcycle, mountaineering) can also affect price.
Can I get life insurance with a pre-existing condition?
Usually yes, but the premium and underwriting class depend on the condition, severity, treatment and stability. Common conditions (controlled hypertension, hypothyroidism, treated depression, history of skin cancer in remission) are routinely accepted at standard or slightly rated premiums. Some carriers are more accommodating than others, which is where an independent broker matters. We submit anonymized inquiries to multiple carriers before recommending an application path.
