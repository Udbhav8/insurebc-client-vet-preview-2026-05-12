Coverage breakdown

Commercial General Liability

$2M or $5M for bodily injury, property damage and personal injury caused by the event. The base coverage every venue will ask for.

Host liquor liability

Covers the host for alcohol-related claims at the event. Required by most BC venues whenever alcohol is served.

Cancellation

Recover non-refundable deposits and prepaid event costs for covered cancellations (weather, illness, vendor failure, venue damage).

Property damage to venue

Pays for accidental damage you or guests cause to the venue (broken fixtures, stained carpets, damaged equipment).

Vendor & participant liability

Coverage for vendors, performers and participants under the event policy (or as additional insureds), depending on the contract structure.

Outdoor / weather risk

For Beacon Hill Park, Cattle Point, Bear Mountain and Sooke Harbour weddings, add a rain plan and cancellation coverage. Vancouver Island weather is unpredictable.

BC LCRB Special Event Permit

If you are serving alcohol at an event that is not at a venue with its own liquor licence, BC's Liquor and Cannabis Regulation Branch requires a Special Event Permit (SEP). The SEP is separate from event insurance, and most venues will also require host liquor liability on top of the SEP. We walk you through both and time the certificate of insurance to your venue's deadline.

FAQ

How much does wedding insurance cost in BC?
Wedding insurance in Victoria typically costs $150-$350 for a 50-300 guest wedding with $2M general liability. Host liquor liability is an add-on that starts around $75 and scales with guest count and alcohol service style (cash bar, open bar, self-pour). Cancellation coverage to recover non-refundable deposits and prepaid trip costs is priced as a percentage of total event cost (commonly 4-8 percent).
Do I need event insurance if my venue has insurance?
Yes. The venue's policy covers the venue's operations and structure, not your event. Most Victoria venues (Royal BC Museum, Hatley Castle, Butchart Gardens, Inn at Laurel Point, Empress, Crystal Garden, Government House) require event organizers to carry their own $2M-$5M CGL and name the venue as additional insured on a certificate of insurance. We issue same-day certificates.
What is host liquor liability in BC?
Host liquor liability covers the host (you) for claims arising from serving alcohol at your event: guests injured after drinking, third-party property damage caused by an intoxicated guest, or alcohol-related incidents during or after the event. It is separate from a venue's commercial liquor licence. If alcohol is served at your Victoria event, host liquor liability is the single most important add-on.
How quickly can I get a certificate of insurance for my venue?
Same day. Once we have the event date, venue name, address and the liability limit your venue requires, we can issue and email the certificate within a couple of hours. We have done this for every major Victoria venue: Royal BC Museum, Crystal Garden, Government House, Hatley Castle, Butchart Gardens, Inn at Laurel Point, Empress Hotel, Victoria Conference Centre, the Bay Centre rooftop and many more.
Does event insurance cover cancellation due to weather?
Yes, if you add cancellation and postponement coverage. The covered reasons typically include severe weather, vendor non-performance, illness, death in family, military deployment, and damage at the venue. Wedding insurance with cancellation costs more than CGL-only event insurance, but for Victoria outdoor weddings (Beacon Hill Park, Cattle Point, Sooke Harbour House, Bear Mountain) it is highly recommended.
Do I need a BC LCRB Special Event Permit?
If you are serving alcohol at an event that is not at a venue with its own liquor licence, yes. The BC Liquor and Cannabis Regulation Branch (LCRB) requires a Special Event Permit (SEP) for most private events where alcohol is served. The SEP is separate from event insurance, and most venues will also require host liquor liability on top of the SEP. We walk clients through both.
