Tenant Insurance in Victoria, BC

Tenant insurance in Victoria starts at $15 per month for basic contents plus $1M liability. Most renters pay $18-$32 per month for $30K contents, $2M liability, and additional living expenses. Built for downtown apartments, student rentals, and Greater Victoria condo rentals.

What a Victoria renters policy covers

  • Contents (furniture, electronics, clothing, bikes, instruments)
  • Personal liability ($1M / $2M / $5M tiers)
  • Additional living expenses if your unit is uninhabitable
  • Identity theft (often included)
  • Off-premises contents (laptop at school, gear in transit)
  • Replacement cost preferred over ACV

Why your landlord's policy does not cover you

Your landlord's building insurance covers the structure only. Everything inside your unit and every dollar of liability you create is yours.

Your stuff

The landlord's policy does not cover your laptop, TV, furniture, clothing or bikes if there is a fire or break-in.

Your liability

If you start a kitchen fire that damages other units, or your bathtub overflows into the unit below, you are personally on the hook without your own policy.

Your hotel bill

If your building is uninhabitable after a covered loss, your landlord is not paying for your hotel. Additional living expenses on your renters policy will.

Strata bylaw requirements

Many Victoria condo buildings (especially downtown new-build) require renters to carry $1M-$2M personal liability as a condition of strata bylaws. Check your lease and ask the landlord for the strata's bylaw section on tenants and insurance, we can match the wording on the quote.

FAQ

What is the average cost of tenant insurance in BC?
Average tenant insurance in BC runs $18-$28 per month ($216-$336 per year) for $30,000 of contents coverage plus $2M personal liability and additional living expenses. Basic policies with $1M liability start around $15 per month. Higher contents amounts, $5M liability, and replacement-cost-on-contents push the number up.
Who is the cheapest for renters insurance?
There is no single cheapest carrier for every Victoria renter. BCAA, Square One, Intact, Aviva and Westland-affiliated insurers all compete on tenant business, and the cheapest depends on building type, contents amount, claims history, and bundling. As a local broker we quote multiple carriers and pick the best fit. Most Victoria renters land between $15 and $32 per month.
Does BC Province give tenant insurance?
No. The province of BC does not provide tenant insurance. Some renters confuse this with provincially-administered programs (MSP for healthcare, ICBC for vehicles), but renters insurance is a private-market product. We arrange it through major BC carriers like BCAA, Square One, Intact, Aviva and Westland-affiliated insurers.
How much is tenant insurance usually?
Most Victoria renters pay $18-$32 per month. Downtown studios and 1-bedrooms start at $15-$22 per month. Saanich and Esquimalt 2-bedrooms run $20-$28. Oak Bay heritage house rentals and larger units run $25-$40. Student renters at UVic, Camosun and Royal Roads typically pay $15-$20 per month for basic coverage.
How much should I pay for tenant insurance?
For most Victoria renters the right policy is $30K-$50K of contents at replacement cost, $2M personal liability, and at least three months of additional living expenses, costing $20-$28 per month. If your strata bylaws require $1M-$2M liability minimum (common in downtown new-build buildings), match that. Going beyond $50K contents only makes sense if your gear, bikes, instruments or wardrobe genuinely cost that much to replace.
How much is $100,000 renters insurance?
A Victoria renters policy with $100,000 of contents coverage at replacement cost plus $2M personal liability typically runs $35-$55 per month, depending on building type, claims history, deductible and bundling. We almost always recommend renters look hard at whether $100K of contents is real, an honest belongings inventory usually lands between $25K and $60K for most renters.
