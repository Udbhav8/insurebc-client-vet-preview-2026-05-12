What a travel policy covers

  • Emergency medical (hospital, doctor, ambulance, evacuation)
  • Trip cancellation (non-refundable deposits)
  • Trip interruption (early return)
  • Baggage loss, theft and delay
  • Flight delay and missed connection
  • Accidental death & dismemberment (AD&D)

Five trip types we quote

Single-trip emergency medical - One defined trip, priced per day. Best for one vacation or business trip per year.
Multi-trip annual - Frequent travellers. Unlimited trips in 12 months, each up to 10/17/30/60 days.
Snowbird (60+ day USA / Mexico) - Long-stay coverage for Arizona, California, Florida, Mexico, Spain. Age caps and stability periods matter.
Visitors to Canada - Parents, in-laws and family on visa or super-visa. $50K-$300K coverage tiers, age caps apply.
Trip cancellation & interruption - Recover non-refundable deposits and prepaid trip costs if you have to cancel or come home early for a covered reason.

Snowbird coverage

Most Victoria snowbirds head to Arizona, California, Florida or Mexico for 60-180 day winter stays. Coverage hinges on age, pre-existing conditions and the stability period (90 or 180 days depending on carrier and age band). We work with every major BC-licensed travel insurer and match the policy that gives you the most generous wording for your specific medical history.

Visitors to Canada

Parents, in-laws and family visiting Victoria on visa or super visa need their own medical coverage; MSP does not extend to non-residents. $50,000-$300,000 coverage tiers are standard. Age caps and pre-existing condition rules apply. Bundle with home insurance if hosting long-term, and confirm your liability limits.

FAQ

How much is travel insurance in BC?
A single-trip emergency medical policy for a healthy 35-year-old Victoria traveller costs $2-$5 per day for a one-week US trip. A 30-day Mexico vacation runs $40-$90 for the same traveller. Snowbird policies (60-180 days) for travellers 60-75 typically cost $400-$1,400 depending on age, pre-existing conditions and stability period. Multi-trip annual plans for frequent travellers run $200-$600 per year. Trip cancellation is priced as a percentage of trip cost (typically 4-8 percent).
Do I need travel insurance if I have BCAA or MSP?
Yes. MSP (BC's provincial health plan) provides very limited coverage outside Canada, often less than $75 per day for hospital costs that can be thousands per day in the US. BCAA membership includes emergency roadside but does NOT include travel medical insurance unless you have explicitly purchased a BCAA Travel plan. Always carry a dedicated travel medical policy for any trip outside BC.
Does travel insurance cover pre-existing conditions?
Most travel insurance covers pre-existing conditions only if they have been stable for a defined period (commonly 90, 180 or 365 days depending on the carrier and age band). 'Stable' means no new medication, no dose change, no new symptoms, and no new treatment. As a broker we walk through the stability test with you and match the policy that gives you the most generous wording for your specific medical history.
What's the difference between single-trip and multi-trip travel insurance?
Single-trip covers one defined trip; you pay per day. Multi-trip annual covers any number of trips in a 12-month period, each up to a maximum trip length (commonly 10, 17, 30 or 60 days). Multi-trip is cheaper than buying single-trip policies four or more times per year. Snowbirds with one long winter trip plus shorter trips often combine a long-stay single-trip with a multi-trip annual for the shorter trips.
